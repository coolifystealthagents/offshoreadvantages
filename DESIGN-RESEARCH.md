# Design research

## Brief

OffshoreAdvantages.com needs to help a buyer judge whether a Philippines staffing role makes sense. The site must stay specific to Filipino talent, avoid public pricing and invented proof, and make its independent inquiry-routing model clear.

Research was completed on desktop on July 21, 2026. I reviewed the live homepages rather than copying isolated screenshots or assets.

## Five direct competitors

### 1. Cloudstaff

URL: https://www.cloudstaff.com/

Cloudstaff uses a white enterprise header, a split hero with a calm copy area and a brighter people-and-technology composition, then a proof strip and portrait-led role grid. Electric blue handles most actions, while yellow is saved for stronger conversion moments. The repeated sequence of claim, proof, action, and role examples is easy to scan. The first viewport gets crowded by cookie, newsletter, and chat controls.

### 2. Penbrothers

URL: https://penbrothers.com/

Penbrothers has the clearest brand system in the set: oversized geometric type, cobalt panels, coral actions, pale-blue card fields, and abstract patterned shapes instead of a stock-photo hero. Its role cards, metric rail, and four-step process are tidy without feeling generic. Coral consistently means direct contact; blue means exploration. Some modules rely too heavily on open card space.

### 3. Remote Staff

URL: https://www.remotestaff.com/

Remote Staff pairs a dark forest green with electric lime and an editorial serif. Professional office photography gives way to a dark infrastructure section, white operational cards, and an interactive comparison tool. The serif and interface sans-serif pairing feels established. In the live desktop capture, weak hero contrast and several empty bands hurt the otherwise strong system.

### 4. Emapta

URL: https://emapta.com/

Emapta takes a dark product-platform direction. Its hero combines people, candidate cards, and interface fragments; pink actions and cyan checks stand out against deep navy. Rounded trust panels and monochrome compliance marks suit an enterprise buyer. The direction is premium, though several lazy-loaded areas appeared blank during inspection.

### 5. Outsourced

URL: https://outsourced.ph/

Outsourced uses a navy hero with a sharp diagonal edge, cyan headline accents, a large custom people illustration, and a restrained pink conversion color. Benefit tiles lead into alternating editorial rows, process cards, role rails, and a strong consultation area. It names the Philippines early and often, and its action hierarchy stays clear across a long page. The cookie panel obscures part of the initial story, but the underlying system is the strongest match for this brief.

## Chosen direction: Outsourced

Outsourced wins because its visual system makes a Philippines staffing proposition feel both specific and premium. The dark navy, cyan information layer, and coral action layer create a simple hierarchy. Angled transitions and rounded modules give the page movement without making it look like a generic software template.

The new homepage adapts those principles, not the competitor's layout or assets. The result is an original "Philippines advantage board":

- a navy hero with an original arched image treatment and local team photo
- a unique compass logo that turns direction and decision-making into the brand motif
- a three-part planning strip focused on location, role scope, and access
- four role briefs framed as advantages with matching controls
- a good-fit / pause-first board that gives the page a genuine point of view
- a four-step handoff sequence
- cyan for planning information and coral only for the main inquiry action
- plain disclosure that this is an independent informational site and qualified inquiries may be routed to a Philippines staffing provider

No competitor copy, code, logo, photography, or branded asset was used.

## Image note

The locally stored hero image shows colleagues in a planning session. The page describes only what can be observed and does not infer anyone's nationality, employer, or profession. The surrounding copy supplies the Philippines staffing context.
